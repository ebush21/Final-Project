Strawberry Microwave Breakfast Bowl

Makes: 1 cake

Measurements Ingredients
1/4 cup Oat bran
2 Tbsp Oat flour
2 Tbsp Buckwheat groats
1 Tbsp Ground flaxseed
1/2 tsp Baking powder
A pinch Salt
1/4 tsp Cinnamon
1/2 tsp Vanilla
2 Tbsp Unsweetened applesauce
1/4 cup Almond milk
1/2 cup Fresh strawberries, diced

Directions:

  1. Mix together the oat bran, oat flour, buckwheat groats, flaxseed, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
  2. Stir in the vanilla, applesauce, and almond milk until all the dry ingredients are incorporated. Gently fold in the diced strawberries.
  3. Spray a microwave safe baking dish or ramekin, and pour batter into dish. Heat for 1 minute and 30 seconds, or until the top is set.
  4. Let cool for 2-3 minutes.

5-Minute Vegetarian Burrito Bowl

Prep Time: 5 minutes Total Time: 5 minutes Yield: 1 bowl

Note: You could add sautéd veggies, shredded chicken, tofu, corn, or pico de gallo, or drizzle your bowl with salsa verde or enchilada sauce, or use pinto or refried beans instead of black beans, or top your bowl with with guacamole instead of sliced avocado...so many possibilities!

Measurements Ingredients
1 cup Cooked brown rice, Instant or Frozen
1/2 cup Black beans, drained and rinsed
2-3 Tbsp, or to taste Salsa
1 Tbsp Plain Greek yogurt
1 Tbsp Shredded cheddar or Mexican-blend cheese
Optional Diced avocado

Directions

  1. Prepare brown rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine rice, black beans, salsa, plain Greek yogurt, and shredded cheese. Microwave on high for 30-60 seconds, or until heated through.
  3. Top with diced avocado, diced tomato, guacamole, pico de gallo, crushed tortilla chips, or other desired toppings.

Meat Loaf in a Mug

TOTAL TIME: Prep/Total Time: 15 min. YIELD:1 servings

Measurements Ingredients
2 Tbsp 2% milk
1 Tbsp ketchup
2 Tbsp quick-cooking oats
1 tsp onion soup mix
1/4 pound lean ground beef
Optional Additional ketchup

Directions

  1. In a small bowl, combine the milk, ketchup, oats and soup mix. Crumble beef over mixture and mix well. Pat into a microwave-safe mug or custard cup coated with cooking spray.
  2. Cover and microwave on high for 3 minutes or until meat is no longer pink and a thermometer reads 160°; drain.
  3. Let stand for 3 minutes. Serve with additional ketchup if desired. Yield: 1 serving.

Editor's Note: This recipe was tested in a 1,100-watt microwave.
